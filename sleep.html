My observations and reflection on sleep

I've been using Whoop to track my sleep, and it's taught me a lot. On average, I need about 7.5 hours of sleep, but to get that, I actually need to be in bed for 8.5 hours because my sleep efficiency is only 85-90%. The quality of my sleep, exercise, and sickness also play a role in how rested I feel, even if I hit my 7.5-hour goal.

I've identified several factors that affect my sleep quality:

Time in bed: I used to think 7 hours in bed was enough, but that only gives me about 6.5 hours of actual sleep. I've realized I need more time in bed to get the sleep I need, especially when I'm active and working out daily.

Consistency: Going to bed at the same time each night helps me fall asleep faster by creating a routine.

Food and drinks: Overeating at dinner, eating right before bed, skipping proper meals, snacking excessively, and drinking kombucha (which has caffeine) in the evening can all negatively impact my sleep quality.

Caffeine timing: The amount and timing of my caffeine intake impact my sleep. I try to cut off caffeine between 1:00-1:30 pm.

Screen time: Using my phone, computer, or watching movies before bed makes it harder to fall asleep and sleep well. Avoiding screens close to bedtime is crucial.

Room temperature and bedding: The temperature in my bedroom, my bedding, and sometimes the extra body heat from loved ones can affect my sleep quality.

Sleep interruptions: My sleep gets disrupted when family members go to bed late, wake up in the middle of the night, or come to my bed.

Morning sunlight: I try to get morning sunlight, but I feel like food, avoiding screens, and time in bed have a more noticeable impact on my sleep quality.

Tracking my sleep has been eye-opening. While sometimes I can feel okay on less sleep, it catches up to me after a day or two. By paying attention to these factors and making changes, I'm working on improving my sleep quality and overall well-being.
